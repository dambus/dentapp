# DentApp — Product Vision

## 1. Product Summary

DentApp is a dental practice management application designed to support the daily operation of a dental practice with multiple doctors.

The initial version will be developed as an internal pilot solution for one real dental practice in Serbia. The long-term goal is to commercialize the application as a SaaS product for dental practices and clinics in Serbia, the region, and potentially international markets.

DentApp should not be treated as a simple calendar, patient list, or digital notebook. The product should connect the most important operational areas of a dental practice into one structured workflow.

---

## 2. Product North Star

DentApp must help the practice know, at any moment:

- what was agreed with the patient,
- what was planned,
- what was performed,
- what was paid,
- what remains unpaid,
- who performed the work,
- what belongs to each doctor,
- what materials are available,
- what materials are missing,
- what needs to happen next.

---

## 3. Initial Target Customer

The initial target customer is:

A single dental practice with multiple doctors, shared patients, shared materials, manual internal scheduling, and a need for better control over patient records, treatment plans, payments, doctor commissions, and inventory.

This model is considered common in Serbia and the region.

---

## 4. Initial Use Case

The initial version is intended for internal practice use only.

The first version should support:

- doctors,
- assistants,
- reception/admin staff,
- owner/practice manager,
- inventory responsible person.

Patient-facing features are not part of the initial MVP.

---

## 5. Core Problem

Many dental practices operate with fragmented information.

Common problems include:

- patient records are still kept on paper,
- treatment plans are often remembered by doctors instead of being clearly documented,
- financial agreements are not always clearly visible,
- patients often pay in installments or later,
- doctor commission is often calculated manually,
- material inventory is shared and poorly controlled,
- responsibility for ordering materials is unclear,
- information is spread between paper, memory, phone calls, Google Calendar, Excel, and informal notes.

DentApp should reduce this operational chaos.

---

## 6. Core Product Idea

DentApp should become a workflow-first dental practice operating system.

The system should connect:

- patient profile,
- medical/dental record,
- odontogram,
- treatment plan,
- appointments,
- visits,
- performed services,
- payments,
- unpaid balances,
- doctor commission,
- inventory,
- material requests,
- reports,
- audit logs.

---

## 7. Main Differentiation

DentApp should differentiate itself from generic dental software by focusing strongly on:

1. Treatment plan management
2. Patient debt and installment tracking
3. Doctor commission calculation
4. Inventory and material responsibility
5. Real-world Serbian/regional dental practice workflows
6. Practical print/export support
7. Clear role-based access and auditability

The goal is not only to store information, but to make daily decisions easier.

---

## 8. Initial MVP Focus

The MVP should focus on internal operational value.

The first MVP should include:

- authentication and user roles,
- patients,
- patient records,
- anamnesis and important notes,
- odontogram,
- treatment plans,
- services and price list,
- appointments,
- visits and performed services,
- patient ledger,
- payments, unpaid balances, installments, and advances,
- doctor commission rules and reports,
- inventory items,
- inventory movements,
- material requests,
- basic reports,
- PDF/print exports,
- audit log.

---

## 9. Not Part of Initial MVP

The following are intentionally excluded from the first MVP:

- patient portal,
- online booking by patients,
- automated SMS/Viber/WhatsApp reminders,
- native mobile apps,
- multi-location support,
- full accounting system,
- fiscalization integration,
- dental laboratory portal,
- marketing automation,
- AI diagnosis,
- automated clinical decision-making.

These can be revisited after the pilot proves the core workflow.

---

## 10. Product Philosophy

DentApp should be:

- practical,
- fast,
- secure,
- structured,
- easy to use during a working day,
- suitable for desktop, tablet, and mobile,
- configurable enough for future SaaS use,
- simple enough for a real dental team to adopt.

DentApp should avoid:

- unnecessary complexity,
- generic template-like UI,
- over-customization for only one pilot practice,
- adding features before the workflow is understood,
- storing sensitive data without proper controls.

---

## 11. Long-Term Vision

Long term, DentApp may become a SaaS platform for dental practices and clinics.

Possible future modules include:

- patient portal,
- online booking,
- reminders,
- digital consent,
- laboratory collaboration,
- advanced reports,
- multi-location management,
- integrations,
- analytics,
- inventory automation,
- regional localization,
- internationalization.

However, the first goal is to build a strong and validated internal workflow system for the pilot practice.
